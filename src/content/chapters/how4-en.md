## 4.1 What Is Making Things Happen

### <span style="color:#C0392B">4.1.0 Making things happen means the elite amplify their original contributions by organizing resources, ultimately changing the world — this is the task line</span>

**4.1.0.1 The highest-efficiency method for accomplishing this task is called the Making Things Happen Method**

<span style="color:#17A2B8">*"To 'make' is to act innovatively"*</span>

<span style="color:#C0392B">**4.1.0.2 The main body of this chapter discusses how to profoundly change the world, hereinafter the Mahayana Making Things Happen Method**</span>

**4.1.0.3 The final section of this chapter discusses how to make incremental innovations that change the world, hereinafter the Hinayana Making Things Happen Method**

a. It should be noted that the two are fundamentally different and cannot be transformed by scaling the concept of "world" up or down
b. <span style="color:#E67E22">**The former's starting point is essentially solving problems; the latter's starting point is satisfying current market demand and capturing era dividends**</span>

### 4.1.1 This chapter only discusses the Making Things Happen Method of the elite (whom others don't yet recognize as elite)

**4.1.1.1 Going from 0 to ε is many times harder than going from ε to 1, especially for the Mahayana Method**

<span style="color:#2E86C1">*Intuitively, the Mahayana Method targets the ultimate goal directly, while the Hinayana Method aims to be recognized by the world as quickly as possible upon reaching "1"*</span>

**4.1.1.2 "How successful people achieve greater success" is a widely circulated field of study; restating it here adds little value**

<span style="color:#2E86C1">*The elite's capabilities grow exponentially; this chapter only discusses the Making Things Happen Method for the first four years (hereinafter "the undergraduate years")*</span>

## ==4.3 Reading / Writing The Paper==

### 4.3.0 Have a frontier domain for deep research / insight

**4.3.0.1 Deep research / insight means**

a. Statically, you have put in the work in this field and have your own understanding of frontier knowledge that has not yet formed market consensus, or even been published
b. Dynamically, this understanding needs to be structural / dynamic; you should have some ability to predict the future in this field
   - <span style="color:#2E86C1">*What are the big problems in this field? Are there any fundamental breakthroughs?*</span>
   - <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Where is it currently stuck? What are the fundamental obstacles? Consequently, what is the most promising route?*</span>
   - <span style="color:#2E86C1">*In the long run, what is definitely achievable? What is impossible to achieve?*</span>

<span style="color:#C0392B">**4.3.0.2 You don't need to be a scientist / researcher, but you must read papers**</span>

<span style="color:#2E86C1">*To search is to research; to have taste is to have mastery*</span>

**4.3.0.3 Enter this field's community, communicate and learn frequently, and selflessly contribute to the community yourself**

<span style="color:#8E44AD">*Great winds arise from the tips of grass; great enterprises originate from interest groups*</span>

### <span style="color:#C0392B">**4.3.1 The Paper provides scientific "existence proof" for The Idea**</span>

**4.3.1.1 The Idea is often proposed before The Paper; it needs a scientifically sound foundation**

**4.3.1.2 The Idea frequently comes from linear extrapolation "imagination" based on existing theories / technologies and user feedback, having some precursory nature**

<span style="color:#8E44AD">*Version-defying business ideas often come from user complaints; era-defining trading insights often come from client calls*</span>

**4.3.1.3 Existence is generally a big problem**

<span style="color:#2E86C1">*"The most important knowledge about the atomic bomb is that it can be built" (Yang Zhenning)*</span>
<span style="color:#2E86C1">*"I've seen many people who broke the law, but I've never seen anyone who could break the laws of physics" (Musk)*</span>

### **4.3.2 The Paper generally only resolves "existence," not the specific path**

**4.3.2.1 If existence is not resolved, the expected time to solve the problem is strictly positive infinity**

**4.3.2.2 Once existence is solid, you're within range of brute-force miracles**

<span style="color:#C0392B">**4.3.2.3 The Paper generally does not provide specific solution steps for the problem**</span>

a. <span style="color:#2E86C1">*If it does, the problem isn't hard enough, and The Idea isn't sufficient to sustain a great company*</span>
b. <span style="color:#2E86C1">*If it does, even if you're among the earlier readers of The Paper — given the undergraduate stage, this probably isn't something you should be doing*</span>

## ==4.4 Think Through The Idea==

### <span style="color:#E67E22">**4.4.1 The Paper makes The Idea "something that is theoretically possible but has not yet happened in practice"**</span>

**4.4.1.1 Always strive to solve problems fundamentally**

<span style="color:#17A2B8">*"Can we do something that surpasses current industry standards, surpasses current product definitions — something more innovative and more valuable?"*</span>

**4.4.1.2 Do the right thing, not the easy thing**

<span style="color:#17A2B8">*"Why, sometimes I've believed as many as six impossible things before breakfast" (*Alice in Wonderland*)*</span>

### **4.4.2 The Idea must pass the Mars Test**

<span style="color:#C0392B">**4.4.2.1 From Mars looking at Earth: should this be done? Should you be the one doing it? In the long run, what will this thing ultimately look like? Would you rather live in a world where this thing happens, or the opposite?**</span>

**4.4.2.2 Even for large companies, most cannot pass the above Mars Test**

<span style="color:#2E86C1">*That is, their existence is due to some historical advantage, not because the world losing them would be efficiency-losing*</span>

<span style="color:#8E44AD">**4.4.2.3 Suffice it to say, efficiency-losing things cannot stably exist in the ultra-long run**</span>

### **4.4.3 Don't start by stitching together initial resources**

**4.4.3.1 Initial resource combinations are random and have no reason to be the logical starting point of a long-term plan**

<span style="color:#C0392B">**4.4.3.2 The Idea's validation requires you / your team to have original endogenous growth capability; exogenous resources may distort this judgment**</span>

a. "Cognition → Judgment → Resource allocation → Execution → Result validation → Cognitive improvement" is hereinafter called the "Practice Flywheel"
b. The Practice Flywheel grows endogenously; exogenous resources can break this flywheel, causing strategic distortion
c. Exogenous resources aren't even free. Being pulled by them forces you to commit matching resources, and during the undergraduate years, precious resources should be used to validate the Practice Flywheel

> <span style="color:#7F8C8D">**4.4.3.3 Not taking shortcuts is the gold standard for business models**</span>
> a. <span style="color:#2E86C1">***Shortcut environments and practices cultivate fake capabilities***</span>

### **4.4.4 An example of The Idea**

> <span style="color:#7F8C8D">**4.4.4.1 Deep information reuse agents**</span>

a. AI has caused easily searchable information to depreciate sharply. Meanwhile, the total value of information is exogenously determined by demand — correspondingly, deep information will appreciate dramatically. The way to obtain the latter is through in-depth interviews. In 2504, after a project is completed, deep information becomes localized because its value is sealed within the overall report (finding / reading the entire report is too costly)
b. <span style="color:#C0392B">**This information is reusable. One possible approach: use AI to aggregate deep information from many completed independent projects into an agent, and open it to pro users for paid chat interactions**</span>

## ==4.7 Brute-Force Miracles for Rapid Iteration, Pragmatic Romance for Stunning Products==
