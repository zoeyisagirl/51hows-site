## 3.0 What Is Maintaining Position

### 3.0.1 Maintaining position means that after reaching W_p, your most important 30 non-kin social relationships/friends (hereafter F30) maintain stable positive expectations of you

a. <span style="color:#C0392B">**This of course requires emotional commitment and long-term effort. Generally, there are two primary task lines: financial security and maintaining dignity**</span>

b. A person can generally only sustain direct emotional connections with ~150 people (Dunbar's number). F30 represents the 20% accounting for 80% of importance among them.

### **3.0.2 The method of maintaining position refers to the techniques for achieving both financial security and maintaining dignity — neither is dispensable. This chapter is divided into 3H1 and 3H2**

<span style="color:#E67E22">**3.0.2.1 Financial security means ensuring that principal is never subject to permanent loss**</span>

a. Concretely: in every medium-term period, net worth must not fall below e⁻¹

b. <span style="color:#2E86C1">*In theory, wealth management methods can recover assets by e× over 7 years, so it suffices to stably keep assets above e⁻¹*</span>

<span style="color:#E67E22">**3.0.2.2 Maintaining dignity means ensuring that F30 will support you in the next important win-win move**</span>

a. <span style="color:#27AE60">**As the Chairman said, "Politics means making as many friends as possible and as few enemies as possible"**</span>

<span style="color:#2E86C1">*Historically, the Chairman placed extraordinary importance on comrades who supported him during adversity.*</span>

b. At the margin, this means that within some social domain, "becoming someone's new friend" is a self-reinforcing conformist behavior rather than anti-conformist behavior.

- <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Further, within any social domain, having a base group that operates on a goodwill assumption toward you is the foundation for making new friends*</span>
- <span style="color:#8E44AD">*If no such group happens to exist, making new friends requires switching to a different social domain — colloquially called "restarting one's reputation"*</span>

## <span style="color:#7F8C8D">==3.1 Perpetual Financial Freedom / Endogenous Financial Security==</span>

### 3.1.0 Mathematical Model of Endogenous Financial Security

**Assume M_i has initial wealth W_i1, where W_i1 ≥ W_H**

**3.1.0.1** <span style="color:#C0392B">**Let M_i's annual total resource consumption (e.g., living expenses, inflation, business risks) be factorized as discount consumption factor R_iD, R_iD ∈ (0, e−1], D for discount; risk-free return rate R_iF, R_iF ∈ (−100%, R_m), F for risk-free**</span>

a. <span style="color:#E67E22">**Factorizing R_iD is reasonable. The resources consumed to maintain the status quo are not fixed but scale with current wealth W_i1**</span>

- <span style="color:#2E86C1">*R_iD = e−1 means consuming down to e⁻¹ per year; larger R_iD values have no meaningful discussion value*</span>

b. <span style="color:#E67E22">**Risk-free return R_iF is an independent variable with its own significance. If M_i takes on business risks, R_iD increases correspondingly**</span>

- <span style="color:#2E86C1">*R_iF can certainly be negative; −100% means losing everything within a year*</span>
- <span style="color:#2E86C1">*R_iF does not exceed R_m; no asset's long-term compound return can exceed R_m*</span>

**3.1.0.2** The subscript *i* is necessary. For different M_i, R_iD/R_iF values differ entirely, even if their W_i1 is identical.

<span style="color:#2E86C1">*Each M_i differs enormously in risk appetite, lifestyle habits, ability, and family culture.*</span>

**3.1.0.3** In year two, W_i2 = W_i1(1+R_iF)/(1+R_iD). Generally, W_i,n+1 = W_i1[(1+R_iF)/(1+R_iD)]ⁿ ...(*)

### **3.1.0.4 If R_iF < R_iD, W_in converges to 0**

a. <span style="color:#8E44AD">**Colloquially "wealth doesn't survive three generations" — empirically, most W_i1 decline to e⁻¹ W_H within 60 years (three decades)**</span>

- <span style="color:#2E86C1">*This universality is independent of W_i1. This implies that generally, the larger W_i1, the lower R_iF — colloquially "rich families produce prodigal children"*</span>

> b. In (*), let W_i1 = W_H, n=60, R_iD=4%, R_iF=2%, then W_55 = e⁻¹ W_H, 3×18 = 54 years to fall below W_H;
> <span style="color:#C0392B">**This is a typical indicator set explaining "why do rationally pragmatic yet non-resilient families lose wealth across three generations?". It can be viewed as the gentlest form of W_H degradation — the Degradation Method**</span>

> <span style="color:#7F8C8D">**==3.1.0.5 Perpetual financial freedom thus requires R_iF ≥ R_iD, and this is achievable==**</span>

### **3.1.1 The first principle of endogenous financial security is maintaining R_iF ≥ R_iD**

a. Why does R_iF ≥ R_iD not violate entropy increase? Because it has real commercial significance.

b. <span style="color:#2E86C1">*That is, once W_H is reached, its risk-free growth rate can resist attrition.*</span>

**3.1.1.1** It is well known that growth comes from innovation/new things/new concepts developing.

<span style="color:#C0392B">**3.1.1.2 For any new thing, establishing and achieving widespread acceptance of its value in era T generally requires endorsement from influential M during era T−1**</span>

<span style="color:#8E44AD">*Colloquially "setting up the table," "building the canopy," "the people back the winner"*</span>

<span style="color:#8E44AD">*Value-system reshaping that does NOT require T−1 era M endorsement is called "value revolution." Value revolutions are rare.*</span>

a. <span style="color:#27AE60">*NFTs being widely recognized as investable required Steph Curry using an NFT ape avatar.*</span>

b. <span style="color:#27AE60">*BTC being widely recognized as investable required large traditional financial institutions and even national central banks including it in asset allocations.*</span>

c. <span style="color:#2E86C1">*NFTs/BTC then appreciated dramatically during this process. What did Curry or central banks need to do?*</span>

d. <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Nothing at all. Their returns were zero-risk. Their contribution to "setting up the table" was indispensable.*</span>

**3.1.1.3** M's existence/participation/endorsement itself carries indispensable milestone value in a new thing/concept's development. Conversely, every new class of assets inevitably produces its own representative new M (or several).

<span style="color:#8E44AD">*Colloquially "seated at the table," "has a say," "the birth of a faction"*</span>

a. <span style="color:#2E86C1">***The essential difference in becoming an M is that other Ms consider you one of their own***</span>

b. <span style="color:#2E86C1">***As long as your money came from legitimate sources, everyone assumes you're capable. As long as you don't sabotage yourself, everyone factors you in as "always being there" when thinking about things***</span>

c. <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Concretely, they'll consult you on matters, reach out when opportunities arise, and if they must harm you they'll give you advance notice.*</span>

### <span style="color:#27AE60">**3.1.2 The threshold for M status in the 2020s is W_H**</span>

**3.1.2.1** It is generally accepted that reaching W_H represents an extraordinary commercial achievement comparable to world-class achievements in other fields without being overshadowed.

<span style="color:#27AE60">*"I want to become an athlete who earns W_H." — LeBron James*</span>

**3.1.2.2** Money is the image of reason and storyline condensed upon you; it is an endogenous variable of reason and storyline.

**3.1.2.3** If you/your family's existence has enduring value, perpetual financial freedom is not an objective but a byproduct.

### **3.1.3 The logical chain above is "if and only if" — meaning its converse theorems also hold**

<span style="color:#8E44AD">**3.1.3.1 Converse theorem I: Below W_H, accessible investment opportunities are not risk-free cake**</span>

a. Colloquially "not seated at the table" — people assume you'll probably lose it all through incompetence.

b. This is the academic definition of old money. Below this threshold, family legacy cannot genuinely exist.

> <span style="color:#7F8C8D">**==3.1.3.2 "How others treat you is priceless." If you happen to not be at W_H, what truly belongs to you is only memories and experiences from every amusement park visited, and the genuine feelings given and received along the way==**</span>

<span style="color:#8E44AD">**3.1.3.3 Converse theorem II: If you or your family's existence lacks enduring value, then W_in > W_H cannot persist long-term**</span>

a. W_H is merely a symbolic expression that you remain at the table, while the table itself is perpetually renewed.

b. <span style="color:#2E86C1">***Mechanical caution/frugality/risk avoidance has limited effect. You cannot preserve through mere protection alone — W_H must carry weight***</span>

- <span style="color:#8E44AD">*If Li Shishi in Water Margin is historically the same person, then Emperor Daojun was Emperor Huizong of Song. That is, within single-digit years after Song Jiang's execution, Huizong was captured by the Jin dynasty.*</span>

### **3.1.4 How to achieve R_iF ≥ R_iD?**

> <span style="color:#7F8C8D">**==3.1.4.0 After reaching W_H, simply "fulfill duties without self-sabotage" suffices==**</span>

<span style="color:#C0392B">**3.1.4.1 "Fulfilling duties without self-sabotage" means being widely perceived as having made maximum effort to preserve one's own value (commercial/social/influence), without slackening, rather than allowing it to casually dissipate**</span>

<span style="color:#2E86C1">*This is a high bar. Some W_H families even codify this requirement in a written document, colloquially "family precepts."*</span>

<span style="color:#C0392B">**3.1.4.2 Not self-sabotaging guarantees R_iF > 0; otherwise it becomes a massive negative**</span>

<span style="color:#2E86C1">*Not self-sabotaging means progress — it's only a question of speed.*</span>

<span style="color:#C0392B">**3.1.4.3 Fulfilling duties makes F30 think you're still capable. Across successive win-win moves, R_iF automatically rises above R_iD**</span>

> <span style="color:#7F8C8D">**==a. In practice, R_iD median roughly equals 4%. Doing nothing (R_iF=0), 25 years to consume down to e⁻¹ or confirmed degradation==**</span>

a. <span style="color:#8E44AD">**Without self-sabotage, beating R_iD is not difficult — colloquially "you may not be fierce yourself, but you always have that one classmate who's pretty fierce"**</span>

<span style="color:#2E86C1">*Boss Jia and Teacher Y both lost their original businesses. But Teacher Y didn't self-sabotage, so his seat remained — he's still seen as a faction head, still in the game.*</span>

### **3.1.5 How does contemporary financial freedom evolve into perpetual financial freedom?**

**3.1.5.1** Concretely, how to achieve W_p → W_H?

<span style="color:#C0392B">**3.1.5.2 This process cannot be completed by the non-elite**</span>

## 3.2 Exogenous Financial Security

### 3.2.0 First Principle of Exogenous Financial Security

**3.2.0.1** If you are an elite W_p, the first principle of exogenous financial security is continuing to serve original dishes to the world.

<span style="color:#2E86C1">*Your original contributions as the driver of sustained wealth growth, not your money's contributions. At the W_p level, money is ordinary.*</span>

<span style="color:#C0392B">**3.2.0.2** If you are a non-elite W_p, the first principle of exogenous financial security is heavy reliance on accessible club investments.</span>

<span style="color:#2E86C1">*Hereafter in this chapter, W_p defaults to non-elite W_p.*</span>

<span style="color:#C0392B">**3.2.0.3 Club investment refers to projects operated by M, with widespread F30 participation, denoted C_l, with participants denoted c_l, CL for Club**</span>

### **3.2.1 How Does W_p Allocate Assets?**

A typical Chinese family office's approximate allocation across asset categories (with standard variance):

**3.2.1.1 e⁻¹ / 37% floor assets**

a. <span style="color:#27AE60">**22% real estate**</span>

- <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Occasionally also allocating to physical assets (oil & gas/gold)*</span>
- <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Chinese people prefer real estate more.*</span>

b. <span style="color:#27AE60">**15% cash/cash equivalents/fixed income**</span>

- <span style="color:#27AE60">*Must always maintain capacity for club investment participation. In fact, due to the existence of club investments, cash is actually the most aggressive asset.*</span>
- <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Due to agency problems with family office managers and the pitfall nature of structured fixed-income products, the only safe fixed-income product is short-term U.S. Treasuries.*</span>

**3.2.1.2 e⁻¹ / 37% alternative assets (offensive assets)**

- <span style="color:#27AE60">**32% alternative assets**</span>
    - PE/VC funds and attached co-investment allocations
        - Becoming a fund investor in multiple PE/VC firms. Beyond collecting financial returns, this can be viewed as joining an intellectually resource-leading club.
        - This is one of the best ways to maintain broad contact with global cutting-edge advancement, second only to reading research papers oneself.
            - <span style="color:#2E86C1">*SQ Fund consistently provides BD secondary-share discount allocations to its investors while locking in its own returns.*</span>
    - Hedge funds/quantitative funds/BTC
- <span style="color:#27AE60">**5% art/annual donation commitments**</span>
    - Building two resumes: one for art disposition and one for charitable giving.

    **3.2.1.3 (1 − 2e⁻¹) / 26% standard assets**

    a. <span style="color:#27AE60">*U.S. equities: MAG7/pharma/Nasdaq; A-shares/HK equities: Kweichow Moutai/Tencent*</span>

### **3.2.2 Club Investment**

**3.2.2.0** Non-elite W_p cannot engage in industrial/industrial investment (or prior industry has failed and transformation is impossible).

**3.2.2.1** Maintaining dignity sustains one's social circle and updates market consensus value.

<span style="color:#C0392B">**3.2.2.2 One then learns of recent C_l opportunities (though not necessarily proactively invited)**</span>

a. <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Group A privatization and delisting, Group B new-track subsidiary spin-off, Giant C pre-IPO final funding round.*</span>

**3.2.2.3** C_l is (a priori) zero-risk for M.

**3.2.2.4** However, due to differences in trading position and information timeliness, C_l is not 100% positively returning for all c_l participants.

a. <span style="color:#8E44AD">**Empirically its return distribution approximates 50% at 2x, 20% at 1x, 30% at 0x, yielding ~90% probability of profit after three compound rounds**</span>

### <span style="color:#C0392B">**3.2.3 Participate in C_l once every few years. With luck, exogenous financial security can be maintained**</span>

<span style="color:#C0392B">**3.2.3.1 Why can't wealth management methods recover R_m after reaching W_p?**</span>

a. Wealth management methods involve highly concentrated holdings. At W_p, there is too much hard-won capital to bear such idiosyncratic risk; black swans must be guarded against.

<span style="color:#2E86C1">*Even Tencent dares not go all-in anymore — diversification starts to make sense.*</span>

b. Wealth management methods require ultra-long-term judgment certainty regarding The Portfolio.

<span style="color:#2E86C1">*No target maintains certainty forever.*</span>

c. <span style="color:#C0392B">**In practice, W_p struggles to concentrate assets in just 2–5 targets**</span>

**3.2.3.2** Per 3.2.1.1, you can only deploy a very small proportion of total assets into C_l investments, aiming to lift overall portfolio R_iF.

a. This demands high returns from C_l. Meanwhile, C_l carries risk — three consecutive wrong bets cannot occur.

b. <span style="color:#E67E22">**But non-elite W_p must accept this risk; occasional extraordinary returns make it worthwhile**</span>

c. <span style="color:#E67E22">**C_l and X_l share the same source, but X_l offers better pricing and higher returns because it monetizes one's own influence rather than mere dependent participation**</span>

### **3.2.4 Two Examples of Club Investment**

**3.2.4.1 Mr. LJC's family investment**

a. <span style="color:#27AE60">*Mr. LJC served as China's wealthiest individual for a long time. After a series of ordinary family investments, he invested in Zoom during the pandemic. By April 2025, Zoom holdings accounted for as much as 1/3 of Mr. L's total assets.*</span>

**3.2.4.2 An internet company's capability-chain spin-off IPO**

a. <span style="color:#27AE60">*After going public, this internet group spun off each operational link of its core business into separate IPOs, all under the original brand.*</span>

b. <span style="color:#27AE60">*By April 2025, five publicly listed companies sharing the brand had been established, with a sixth imminent. Each would have been profitable if invested in from spin-off through IPO.*</span>

c. <span style="color:#27AE60">*If you happen to be in the founder's social circle of this internet group, you could effortlessly maintain position through these opportunities.*</span>

### **3.2.5 It should be noted that "club project" is a logical concept whose form need not even be investment**

a. <span style="color:#C0392B">**Club projects refer to win-win moves generated by F30 participating in designated projects initiated by M**</span>

<span style="color:#2E86C1">*This is a litmus test for M: projects they initiate will definitely proceed (generally within 18 months) regardless of F30 participation levels. Whereas W_p cannot initiate/pursue projects opposed by their entire F30, and day-to-day activities lack predetermined certainty.*</span>

b. Any project whose expected return exceeds market rates qualifies as a club project, colloquially "dependent participation"/"licking the pot rim." Non-investment "club projects" (e.g., participating in recruitment or appearing in new productions) are omitted here.

> <span style="color:#7F8C8D">**==3.2.6 Before W_p, long-term financial security does not exist, hence position maintenance is unnecessary==**</span>
>
> <span style="color:#7F8C8D">**==3.2.6.1 Before W_p / wealth management success, R_iD greatly exceeds R_iF==**</span>
>
> a. Note that R_iD is a factor. When asset scale is small, fixed living expenses produce an R_iD far exceeding 4%.
>
> b. <span style="color:#2E86C1">*On the other hand, before reaching W_p, low probability of accessing club investments means R_iF approaches zero.*</span>
>
> <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Note that wage income and investment income are both not risk-free; increasing risk-return inevitably synchronously increases R_iD.*</span>
>
> c. The above analysis targets expectation; in reality, variance plays a larger role. One blowout event ends the game.
>
> - <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Those between W_F and W_p are hereafter called the middle class.*</span>
> - The middle class cannot withstand one serious illness, unemployment, divorce, investment failure, or other life accident...
>     - <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Cannot withstand means permanent loss of principal, falling below e⁻¹.*</span>
> - Over ultra-long horizons, these negative events inevitably arrive.
>     - <span style="color:#8E44AD">*Thus the middle class is not a state variable with stable meaning but a temporary probabilistic manifestation.*</span>
>
>     **3.2.6.2** In summary, the middle class possesses no meaningful concept of financial security.
>
>     <span style="color:#E67E22">**3.2.6.3 The rational approach is applying wealth management and shore-reaching methods, bearing necessary (concentrated investment) risks, and reaching shore as early as possible**</span>

# 3H2. Maintaining Dignity

> <span style="color:#7F8C8D">**==3.3 Maintaining dignity means doing everything possible to prevent F30 from forming negative suspicions or unnecessary conjectures==**</span>

<span style="color:#C0392B">**3.3.1 The first principle of maintaining dignity is: never offend those truly beyond offending (collectively termed M alongside the aforementioned M)**</span>

**3.3.2 Those truly beyond offending are individuals whose unilateral opinion can alter F30's baseline goodwill assumption toward you**

---

## 3.4 Core Elements of Maintaining Dignity

### <span style="color:#C0392B">**3.4.1 Core element I of maintaining dignity: Social vigilance**</span>

**3.4.1.1** M always exists.

a. The wealthier one is, the greater the need for cautious real-time situational judgment of one's social environment.

<span style="color:#8E44AD">*Colloquially "having situational awareness."*</span>

b. Long-term probability of offending M is high.

<span style="color:#2E86C1">*Relatively speaking, the probability of getting three consecutive club investment bets wrong is quite low.*</span>

<span style="color:#27AE60">**3.4.1.2 Lenin said, all things are universally interconnected.**</span>

a. You don't know what M looks like when they appear.

b. Actions you take to maintain relationship with M_i may offend M_j or even unknown M₊ (M that M needs to maintain for position preservation).

c. You don't know which individuals connected to M (hereafter m) influence M.

d. People's mindsets vary wildly; you don't know how certain moves might trigger thoughts in M/m.

> <span style="color:#7F8C8D">**==3.4.1.3 The direct corollary: unless necessary, avoid dealing with many M simultaneously==**</span>
>
> <span style="color:#2E86C1">*In practice, many W_H interact with only one M per phase. Switching M is colloquially "changing allegiance."*</span>

### <span style="color:#E67E22">**3.4.2 Core element II of maintaining dignity: Treat others generously**</span>

**3.4.2.1** This is virtue; doing the right thing is reason enough in itself.

**3.4.2.2** This is also a direct corollary of 3.4.1: treating people sincerely and generously makes it easier to earn understanding and support from M and m.

### <span style="color:#E67E22">**3.4.3 Core element III of maintaining dignity: "When in Rome, do as Romans do"**</span>

**3.4.3.0** "When in Rome, do as Romans do" refers to a social assimilation strategy of proactively integrating into F30's mainstream lifestyle patterns.

**3.4.3.1** This is obvious: non-conformity triggers unnecessary suspicions and conjectures.

**3.4.3.2** You must maintain emotional bonds with F30 anyway; being similar to them across dimensions saves effort over the long term.

**3.4.3.3** This explains why in the 2020s you need to build two resumes: one for art disposition and one for charitable giving.

> <span style="color:#7F8C8D">**==3.4.4 Core element IV of maintaining dignity: "Marital happiness"==**</span>
>
> **3.4.4.1** This is a direct corollary of 3.4.3. Spouse = most important F30 member, hereafter "F1."
>
> **3.4.4.2** F1 ideally shares your interests and stays within mutual expectations. This also falls within F29 expectations, saving effort.
>
> a. Success or failure of most short-to-medium-term goals changes your life by at most x%.
>
> <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Professional/industry choice is 10x%; city/work/promotion are all x%.*</span>
>
> b. Marriage represents a 50% change. In corporate finance terms, you issued 100% new equity and swapped it for F1's 100%.
>
> <span style="color:#8E44AD">*Your lives had no intersection in the first half. Suddenly your decade of study/natural brilliance/leadership acquired exchangeable value, swapable for F1's decade of study/bright intellect/distinguished lineage — precious beyond measure, cherish it.*</span>
>
> **3.4.4.3** If this is not the case, revert to "treat others generously" — i.e., exercise high fault tolerance toward your F1.
>
