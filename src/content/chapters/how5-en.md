## 5.1 What Is Predicting the Future

**5.1.1 A "Qualitative Future Proposition" is a claim, within some applicable system, that a target object will possess a certain qualitative property at a future point in time — abbreviated as "QLFP".**

### **5.1.2 Some conventions: 01-j, Mahayana / Hinayana 01 Method, L1, L0**

<span style="color:#C0392B">**5.1.2.0 This chapter discusses only long-term 0/1 judgments about QALFPs — abbreviated as "01-j", for 0/1 judgment.**</span>

a. 01-j means: for a QLFP in a sufficiently distant and span-unspecified future, give a 0 or 1 judgment on whether it will ultimately hold true
b. 01-j does not predict specific timing
   <span style="color:#2E86C1">*i.e., assume the time span is long enough for the driver to fully play out*</span>
c. 01-j does not specify degree
   <span style="color:#2E86C1">*i.e., no quantitative targets (e.g., adoption rate reaching e⁻¹); only a 0 or 1 output*</span>
d. <span style="color:#E67E22">**Empirically, most truly important long-term judgments are 01-j**</span>
   - <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Chapter 0: Am I an elite person? Should I follow the elite development path, or the non-elite one?*</span>
   - <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Chapter 1: Is this company a good business? Is management trustworthy? Is the extinction risk strictly zero?*</span>
   - <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Chapter 2: In a potential X₂ deal, is the incoming White Knight ultimately reliable?*</span>
   - <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Chapter 3: Who is the M with direct influence over me?*</span>
   - <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Chapter 4: Is The Idea solid enough?*</span>

<span style="color:#C0392B">**5.1.2.1 The best practice principles for 01-j are collectively called the "01 Method".**</span>

a. <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Of course there is no theoretically optimal or certain prediction method — only a set of powerful methodologies grounded in known practice*</span>
b. <span style="color:#2E86C1">*This is fundamentally because the world has infinite dimensions, and progress in modeling it is never-ending*</span>

<span style="color:#C0392B">**5.1.2.2 The 01 Method is divided into the Mahayana 01 Method and the Hinayana 01 Method.**</span>

a. The Mahayana 01 Method refers to the technique of proposing a more fundamental description and dynamics for the system in which the QLFP resides — thereby making 01-j for all QALFPs within that system
   - <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Foundational works of this kind include The Wealth of Nations, Das Kapital, Principles of Economics, The General Theory, Capitalism and Freedom, The Denationalization of Money…*</span>
   - <span style="color:#2E86C1">**In popular business writing, roughly once a decade a work reaches this level: The Black Swan, Zero to One, <span style="color:#1F4E79">LAC</span>**</span>
b. The Hinayana 01 Method refers to the technique of making 01-j on individual QALFPs within a system, without rewriting the system's current description and dynamics
   - <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Obviously, only thinkers and futurists can complete the Mahayana 01 Method; the Hinayana 01 Method is the more practically needed skill for ordinary people*</span>
   - <span style="color:#2E86C1">*In practice, 2504 elite people — busy as they are building things — typically only absorb Mahayana 01 frameworks, and personally perform Hinayana 01-j to guide their work (see below)*</span>

<span style="color:#C0392B">**5.1.2.3 A QLFP that has not yet occurred but will certainly be confirmed in the long run is called "Longterm 1", abbreviated L1. Correspondingly, a QLFP that has not yet dissipated but will certainly be falsified in the long run is called L0.**</span>

a. Below, L0/L1 for the same QLFP will be marked with (0)/(1); unless otherwise noted, the default prediction horizon is year 2504
b. It should be noted that L1 and L0 do not exhaust the space of QALFPs. <span style="color:#8E44AD">**In fact, for most QALFPs, the long-term outcome is neither definitively L1 nor definitively L0.**</span>
c. <span style="color:#E67E22">Both L1 and L0 are extremely strong forms of 01-j</span>

**5.1.2.4 For accessibility, most 01-j examples in this chapter concern economic QALFPs; other domains are touched on less.**

<span style="color:#2E86C1">*The methodology of this work applies equally to sociology, anthropology, international relations, political science, psychology, and natural science.*</span>

### 5.2 Some Known Prediction Techniques

<span style="color:#2E86C1">*For accessibility, only a brief gloss is given here, without extended discussion.*</span>

**5.2.1 Delphi Method**
<span style="color:#2E86C1">*Collect independent expert opinions on a QLFP, consolidate and feed them back to each expert, and ask them to revise their views in light of others' input — repeat until consensus is reached.*</span>

**5.2.2 Scenario Analysis**
<span style="color:#2E86C1">*Enumerate optimistic / baseline / pessimistic scenarios and analyze each.*</span>

**5.2.3 Trend Extrapolation**
<span style="color:#2E86C1">*Analyze trends and project them forward.*</span>

**5.2.4 Simulation Modeling**
<span style="color:#2E86C1">*Use mathematical models and computing technology to simulate complex systems.*</span>

**5.2.5 System Dynamics Analysis**
<span style="color:#2E86C1">*A special type of model: draw causal loop diagrams and build dynamic equations for simulation.*</span>

**5.2.6 Cross-Impact Analysis**
<span style="color:#2E86C1">*Build a matrix to analyze how various factors influence one another.*</span>

**5.2.7 Morphological Analysis**
<span style="color:#2E86C1">*Decompose a subject into component dimensions, enumerate possible states for each, and analyze combinations.*</span>

### <span style="color:#1F4E79">**5.4.2 LAC's ND on traditional scarcity economics and commodity money**</span>

<span style="color:#C0392B">**5.4.2.1 Wealth is knowledge**</span>

a. Except for a small amount consumed by nuclear reactions, atoms have not changed from the Stone Age to the present — wealth comes from advances in how we use them
b. The first principle of economics is not the grim scarcity of matter, but mechanisms that stimulate new knowledge, accelerate learning, and encourage innovation

<span style="color:#C0392B">**5.4.2.2 Growth is learning**</span>

a. This follows directly from the above. In practice, Moore's Law and its theoretical foundation — the "learning curve" — have brought unimaginable gains in efficiency and wealth (<span style="color:#27AE60">over 50+ years, calculations per second have grown by a factor of one million, and memory density by two billion</span>)
b. The silicon and aluminum underlying the semiconductor and optical industries are abundant elements in the earth's crust. Continuous learning and ever-steeper learning curves have created incredible growth and progress

<span style="color:#C0392B">**5.4.2.3 Money is time**</span>

a. Per Gödel's incompleteness theorem, a self-referential system cannot be simultaneously consistent and complete
   <span style="color:#2E86C1">*If money is itself a commodity, the economic system cannot be stable or high-signal*</span>
b. In practice, this flaw in money — its own value fluctuating wildly — is catastrophically efficiency-destroying
   <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Currently, the forex market is the world's single largest market, with annual transaction volume roughly 70x global GDP*</span>
c. <span style="color:#E67E22">Only time, based on universal constants, is the most suitable unit of currency</span>
d. Goods can be priced in time — i.e., how much of your working time this item is equivalent to
   <span style="color:#2E86C1">*This would be clean and elegant; economic quantities would no longer require disputed inflation / PPP adjustments across periods*</span>

<span style="color:#C0392B">**5.4.2.4 Information is surprise**</span>

a. **Surprising information is the only information with value**
   - <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Working on deciphering military codes, Shannon realized that in most communication, only the surprising, unpredictable portion is necessary. The information we need to learn is surprising information.*</span>
   - <span style="color:#2E86C1">*One necessary consequence of applying Shannon's entropy theory to economics: it places high-entropy information on a low-entropy carrier*</span>
b. **In economics, the low-entropy carrier consists of legal rules, property rights, and constitutional freedoms**
   - <span style="color:#2E86C1">*As the lifeblood of the economy, money itself should be low-entropy*</span>
c. **Learning curves and entrepreneurial innovation are the core economic activities**
   - <span style="color:#2E86C1">*Traditional economics holds that all economic systems tend toward "equilibrium," and that pre-equilibrium entrepreneurship is merely the entropy decay toward equilibrium*</span>
   - <span style="color:#C0392B">**Yet obviously, entrepreneurial innovation is not noise in an equilibrating process — it is the true source of value and growth**</span>
